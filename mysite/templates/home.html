Helene The Dog Walker

  • Our Why

    While you're busy, hard at work, your dog will be out and about enjoying a fun-filled adventure around the parks and trails offered in Wellington. These walks help to meet your dog's needs of socialisation, exercise, enrichment and training so that they head home happy and healthy.

  • Structure

    The walks are usually a mixture of walking as a pack and free time. Offleash freedom comes when there is a level of trust between your dog and I, and they understand the expectations and boundaries of the pack.

  • Safety

    Saftey is the top priority on every walk (the second being ensuring the dogs enjoy their walk ofcourse!). I am fully insured and trained in pet first aid. While in the car, the dogs are always crated or tethered. So I can trust them to listen when it counts we invest a lot of time into reinforcing the basics (like stay, come and sticking with the pack).

  • Experienced Dog Handler

    From taking care of hundreds of dogs, as a petsitter, SPCA volunteer, and assistant dog trainer, I have the hands-on experience and knowledge needed to make sure everyone is having a fun time. Alongside this, I have completed courses/certifications in pet first aid and managing dog play.

  • Requirements

    All dogs start with solo walks and progress to the group walks when they are ready. Adding a dog to the group too early would not be enjoyable for them or the other dogs. You must be able to commit to atleast one day a week regularly. This helps to keep the packs stable and means your dog can develop a stronger bond with the other dogs. Your dog must be friendly, neutered (entire females are welcome) and at least 12 months old.
